# Matrix Multiplication

---

## Overview

The product $AB$ is defined when the **inner dimensions match**: if $A$ is $m\times n$ and $B$ is $n\times p$, then $AB$ exists and is $m\times p$. Order matters; generally $AB \ne BA$.

## Definition

For $A \in \mathbb{R}^{m\times n}$ and $B \in \mathbb{R}^{n\times p}$ the product $C=AB$ has entries

$$
c_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_{ik} b_{kj}.
$$

## Algorithm (Row-by-Column)

1. Check: columns$(A)=\,$rows$(B)$. If not, stop (undefined).
2. Initialize an $m\times p$ matrix $C$ with zeros.
3. For each $i=1..m$ and $j=1..p$:
   - Accumulate $c_{ij} += a_{ik} b_{kj}$ for $k=1..n$.
4. Return $C$.

## Worked Example (Shape Change)

Let

$$
A=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & 0 \\ 3 & -1 & 4\end{bmatrix} \quad (2\times 3),\qquad
B=\begin{bmatrix}2 & 5 \\ 1 & 0 \\ -2 & 3\end{bmatrix} \quad (3\times 2).
$$

Inner dimensions match (3). Result will be $2\times 2$.

Compute each entry (row of $A$ dot column of $B$):

$$
\begin{aligned}
c_{11} &= 1\cdot2 + 2\cdot1 + 0\cdot(-2) = 2 + 2 + 0 = 4, \\
c_{12} &= 1\cdot5 + 2\cdot0 + 0\cdot3 = 5 + 0 + 0 = 5, \\
c_{21} &= 3\cdot2 + (-1)\cdot1 + 4\cdot(-2) = 6 - 1 - 8 = -3, \\
c_{22} &= 3\cdot5 + (-1)\cdot0 + 4\cdot3 = 15 + 0 + 12 = 27.
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus

$$
AB=\begin{bmatrix}4 & 5 \\ -3 & 27\end{bmatrix}.
$$

## Non-Commutativity

Using $X=\begin{bmatrix}1&2\\0&1\end{bmatrix}$, $Y=\begin{bmatrix}0&1\\0&0\end{bmatrix}$:

$$
XY=\begin{bmatrix}0&1\\0&0\end{bmatrix},\qquad
YX=\begin{bmatrix}0&0\\0&0\end{bmatrix},\quad XY\ne YX.
$$

## Pitfalls

- Ignoring dimension rule (trying $2\times 3$ times $2\times 2$).
- Assuming commutativity.
- Mixing up row vs column indices in the summation.

## Summary

Multiply only when inner sizes match; each output entry is a dot product of a row of $A$ and a column of $B$.
